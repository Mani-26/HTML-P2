Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Visionary scientist, inspiring leader, and the People's President of India.

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam life:

    1931: Born on October 15th in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. 1960: Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO). 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and played a key role in India's space program. 1980: Appointed as the Chief Executive of Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP). 1992: Became the Scientific Adviser to the Indian Minister of Defense. 1999: Served as the Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India. 2002-2007: Elected as the 11th President of India, becoming popularly known as the People's President. 2011: Launched the "What Can I Give?" movement, encouraging the youth to contribute to the betterment of society. 2015: Passed away on July 27th, leaving behind a legacy of scientific achievements, leadership, and inspiration.
